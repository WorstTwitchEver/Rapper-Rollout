Significant Lyrics

" First things first: rest in peace Uncle Phil
For real
You the only father that I ever knew
I get my bitch pregnant, I'ma be a better you ... "

( No Role Modelz | 2014 Forest Hills Drive )


Uncle Phil is a character in the show "The Fresh Prince of Bel-Air" played by
James Avery who died on December 31st, 2013, one year before the song's release.
To Cole, he was his father-figure; the person he looked up to because he didn't
have anyone to look up to, and promises he'll do better than he did for him growing up.


" Spiralin' up, just like a rich n***a staircase (Spiral up)
No fly zone, please stay the fuck out my airspace (Out my face)
N***as say things behind backs that they wouldn't dare say (Dare say)
Know it's on sight when I see you, I'm workin' at Squarespace "

( m y . l i f e ft. 21 Savage and Morray | The Off Season )


J Cole pokes fun at the rich people who got rich because they were born into a rich family.
They didn't have to struggle and take on challenges like him and instead of being able to
take shortcuts. Because Cole is so prominent, he advises others to not be in his lane otherwise
he'll shoot them down. Hard. On the last part he makes a bar about SquareSpace, something I will
personally use for my Design 1 portfolio project :)


" 1985, I arrived
33 years, damn, I'm grateful I survived
We wasn't s'posed to get past 25
Joke's on you motherfucker, we alive "

( 1985 | KOD )


At the time of this album being released, Cole was 33 years old. Due to racial discrimination,
abusing various drugs, and participating in gang violence, many black men don't see the light of day
past 25 years old. Cole shows gratitude for this because even he didn't know that he'd still be alive.


" Power, greed
Money, Molly, weed
Percs, Xannys, lean, fame
And the strongest drug of them all
And the strongest drug of them all
Love "

( KOD | KOD )


Cole lists off various addictive substances which makes sense because of what the cover art depicts
(kids doing drugs), the content within the songs itself, and even the release date of the album which was
on 4/20, a day that's culturally known for smoking weed. Each of the words spoken describe
each song's main tone.
