Significant Lyrics

" I still see your shadows in my room
Can't take back the love that I gave you
It's to the point where I love and I hate you ... "

( Lucid Dreams | Goodbye and Good Riddance )


"Lucid Dreams" is undoubtedly the most popular song to come from Juice, it would be played and sung everywhere
by everyone, and years later it got a remix with the artist Lil Uzi Vert. The shadows refer to figures people
see during sleep paralysis and in the end, Juice represents the line "It's to the point where I love and I hate you ..."
with friends, family member(s), your relationship partner, or anyone close, as people you hate due to their behavior but
still show love to them because they're still family at the end of the day. After being around them for so long, it's hard
to completely get rid of them in your life.


" Shorty like a thousand dollar plate, fine china
Tell her that she beautiful every day, I remind her... "

( Fine China | WRLD on Drugs )


Juice calls his girl "Fine China", which is a ceramic material that is beautiful and expensive. It's something that is only
used for very special occasions because of its extreme quality, compared to the average plates that people use to eat
their meals on.


" Fuck you in the Prada, poke her face like Lady Gaga
Ride it like a Mazda, zoom, zoom, zoom "

( Flaws and Sins | Death Race for Love )


If you don't know already, Prada is a luxury designer brand and Juice asserts that his girl will be decked in Prada, even during
sexual intercourse. "poke her face like Lady Gaga" is a play on words to Lady Gaga's famous song "Poker Face". Finally, the last
part has 2 meanings, to where he says he'll ride his girl like he's riding in a Mazda, and ends it off with "zoom zoom zoom" because
slogan for Mazda's brand is "zoom zoom". Later in his posthumous album "Legends Never Die", he mentions Prada again in the song
"Up Up and Away" (my personaly favorite on the album).


" Cupid did a drive-by, shot me from the sunroof
Hit a n***a ten times, Gucci shirt, I bled through
Stains, stains, stains, stains, there's blood in the rain
Shame, shame, shame, shame
He used my chest as a firing range "

( Screw Juice | Legends Never Die )


Juice throws a twist on Cupid and says that he's destroyed him because he made him fall in love. The mythology of Cupid is that
he's the Roman God of Love who can make people fall in love with others instantly by shooting them with his arrows. This can also be
remembered in Dro Kenji's song "KILL CUPID" ft. $NOT as well as reminisced in the song "Who Shot Cupid?" in the "Death Race for Love" album.
