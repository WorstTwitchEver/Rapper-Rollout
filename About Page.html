The Rationale of Rapper Rollout

Rapper Rollout is a "for-fun" project made by Daljit Liyal showcasing his admiration for rap artists. It's also made for other people to see their
favorite artists and for people to discover these artists.The black, white, yellow, and purple color palette was thought to give a youthful, modern,
and playful aesthetic. The logo, favicon, and illustrations of each rapper were all designed by Daljit. Genius was used for showcasing the
album art as well as being a valuable source for all of the lyrical content picked in this project. (Of course, Genius is the way to go to understand
lyrical content, but on Rapper Rollout it's just a quick way of getting the jist of things.) Hochelga's video "Survival Guide to the Biblical Apocalypse"
on YouTube going in-depth with the explanation of the judgement day (see Kendrick Lamar's page for more context). Also major props to my brother Harjit
for helping me make my visual ideas come to fruition through the help of coding.

Why Only 8 Artists?

Is what you might be asking... Ok, look: Rapper Rollout was originally intended to only showcase 8 artists, however, it was definitely thought of during
the conceputalizing phase to include more. Other artists like Playboi Carti, The Kid LAROI, Lil Tecca, Young Thug, NAV,
Ian Diorr, 24kGolden, and even more were thought of to join the roster, but with the time it takes to finish just one artist's section, time
restraints have played a huge factor in this project. But in conclusion, I hope the person reading this has enjoyed the Rapper Rollout project!
